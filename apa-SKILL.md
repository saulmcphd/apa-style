# APA Style (7th Edition)

Apply these guidelines when the user requests APA style, paper format, or referencing. Based on the *Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, Seventh Edition* (2020).

---

## Style and Grammar Guidelines

### Voice and Clarity
- Prefer **active voice** unless the actor is unknown or emphasis belongs on the object (e.g., "Participants completed the survey" not "The survey was completed by participants").
- Be **concise**: remove redundancy and unnecessary words.
- Use **continuity and flow**: parallel structure in lists, consistent terminology, clear transitions.
- Use first person ("I" or "we") when describing your own actions or perspective.

### Grammar
- **Subject-verb agreement**: collective nouns (e.g., "group") take singular verbs when the group acts as one; plural when members act individually.
- **Verb tense**: past for procedures and results ("participants completed"); present for general facts and conclusions ("the results indicate"); present perfect for actions beginning in the past and continuing ("researchers have shown").
- **Pronoun agreement**: use "they" as singular when appropriate for gender-inclusive language; ensure clear antecedents.

### Word Choice and Bias-Free Language
- Use **specific, concrete** terms; avoid colloquialisms in formal writing.
- **Bias-free language**: avoid implying superiority, use person-first language (e.g., "person with diabetes"), use current preferred terms for age, disability, gender, race/ethnicity, sexual orientation, and socioeconomic status. Do not use "subjects"; use "participants" or "respondents."

### Punctuation and Mechanics
- **Serial comma**: always use the serial (Oxford) comma (e.g., "A, B, and C").
- **Hyphenation**: hyphenate compound adjectives before a noun (e.g., "risk-adjusted outcomes"); do not hyphenate after "very" or -ly adverbs.
- **Numbers**: spell out numbers below 10 (except in statistics, ages, dates, times, scores, and with units of measurement like "5 mg"); use numerals for 10 and above. Numbers that begin a sentence, title, or heading are always spelled out regardless of value.
- **Abbreviations**: spell out on first use, then use abbreviation; common ones (e.g., APA, IQ) need not be spelled out. Spell out abbreviations again if they first appear in a new major section.

## Paper Format

### General
- **Font**: 12 pt Times New Roman, 11 pt Arial or Calibri, or 11 pt Georgia.
- **Margins**: 1 in (2.54 cm) on all sides.
- **Line spacing**: Double-space the entire manuscript including title page, abstract, body, references, tables, figures, and appendices.
- **Alignment**: Left-aligned (ragged right); **first-line indent** 0.5 in for each paragraph. No extra space between paragraphs.
- **Page numbers**: Top right of every page, beginning on page 1 (the title page). Same font and size as body text. No other header information is required (e.g., no author last names).

### Title Page (Student)
- Title: bold, centered, title case. Positioned 3-4 lines below the top margin of the page.
- One double-spaced blank line before the author name(s).
- Author name(s): first name, middle initial(s), last name. Omit professional titles and degrees (Dr., PhD, etc.).
- Department and institution affiliation.
- Course number and name.
- Instructor name.
- Assignment due date.
- Each element on its own line, centered, double-spaced. No running head unless the instructor requires one.

### Title Page (Professional)
- Running head: optional in 7th edition for most contexts; required only if an instructor or journal requests it. If used, appears as a shortened title in ALL CAPS, flush left in the header.
- Title: bold, centered, title case. Positioned 3-4 lines below the top margin.
- One double-spaced blank line before the author byline.
- Author name(s): first name, middle initial(s), last name. If authors represent multiple institutions, use superscripted numbers to link authors to affiliations. If all authors share one institution, omit the numbers.
- Affiliation(s): department and institution, each on its own centered line.
- **Author Note** (if needed): appears in the lower half of the title page. Bold, centered heading "Author Note." Contains (each as its own indented paragraph, in this order): (1) ORCID iDs, (2) changes of author affiliation, (3) disclosures and acknowledgments, (4) contact information. Omit any part that does not apply.

### Abstract
- Starts on page 2. Heading: **Abstract** (bold, centered).
- Single paragraph, no first-line indent. Typically 150-250 words (check journal or instructor requirements).
- Summarize purpose, methods, results, and conclusions. By convention, abstracts do not normally contain citations.
- **Keywords**: on the line after the abstract, indented 0.5 in. Begin with the label *Keywords:* (italicized, followed by a colon). List keywords in lowercase (except proper nouns), separated by commas, with no period at the end.

### Body
- Starts on page 3 (or the page after the abstract).
- **Repeat the paper title** (bold, centered, title case) at the top of the first page of the body text. Do NOT use an "Introduction" heading; text begins directly under the repeated title.
- **Headings** (use up to five levels as needed):
  - **Level 1**: Centered, Bold, Title Case
  - **Level 2**: Flush Left, Bold, Title Case
  - **Level 3**: Flush Left, Bold Italic, Title Case
  - **Level 4**: Indented 0.5 in, Bold, Title Case, Ending With a Period. Text begins on the same line.
  - **Level 5**: Indented 0.5 in, Bold Italic, Title Case, Ending With a Period. Text begins on the same line.

### Tables and Figures
- Number tables and figures separately and sequentially (Table 1, Table 2; Figure 1, Figure 2).
- **Label**: flush left, bold (e.g., **Table 1** or **Figure 1**).
- **Title**: on the next line, flush left, italic, title case (e.g., *Illustration of HET's Branching Structure*).
- **Notes** (optional): appear below the table or figure. Begin with the label *Note.* (italic, followed by a period). General notes come first, then specific notes indicated by superscripted lowercase letters.
- Tables should be clean with minimal borders and no shading unless shading conveys meaning (explained in the note). If a table spans multiple pages, repeat column headings on each new page.
- Tables and figures can appear in the body near their first mention, or in appendices at the end. Reference them in the text (e.g., "see Table 1" or "as shown in Figure 2").

### Block Quotations
- Quotations of 40 or more words: format as a block quotation. Indent the entire passage 0.5 in from the left margin, with no quotation marks.
- For parenthetical citations, place the page numbers after the closing punctuation of the block: ... local context. (pp. 8-9)
- If the author/date are already given in the narrative, place only the page number after the closing punctuation.

### References
- Start on a new page. Heading: **References** (bold, centered). Use "Reference" (singular) if there is only one entry.
- Double-space all entries. Use hanging indent (0.5 in) for each entry. Do not use tabs for hanging indents if your word processor can create them via formatting settings.
- Alphabetize by first author's surname. Entries by different first authors sharing the same surname are ordered by first authors' given-name initials. Entries with the same full author list are ordered chronologically (n.d. first, then dated, then in press). Entries with the same author list and same year are ordered alphabetically by title (ignoring articles "A," "An," "The") and given lowercase letter suffixes (2020a, 2020b).
- Organizational authors are alphabetized by organization name (ignoring articles). Entries with no credited author are alphabetized by title (ignoring articles).
- Every source cited in text must appear in the reference list, and every reference list entry must be cited in the text. Exception: personal communications are cited in text only (no reference entry).

### Appendices
- Each appendix starts on a separate page. Label as **Appendix** (or **Appendix A**, **Appendix B**, etc. if multiple), bold and centered. Appendix title on the next line, also bold and centered.
- If an appendix contains only a single table or figure, the "Appendix" label replaces the usual table/figure label.
- If an appendix contains both text and tables/figures, label them with the appendix letter (e.g., Table A1, Table A2, Figure A1; or Table B1 for Appendix B).
- Paragraphs in appendices follow normal body formatting (indented, double-spaced).

## Referencing

### In-Text Citations

#### Basic Forms
- **Author-date system**: parenthetical form (Author, Year) or narrative form Author (Year).
- Use **ampersand (&)** in parenthetical citations: (Smith & Jones, 2020). Use **"and"** in narrative citations: Smith and Jones (2020).

#### Author Variations
- **One author**: (Smith, 2020) / Smith (2020).
- **Two authors**: (Smith & Jones, 2020) / Smith and Jones (2020).
- **Three or more authors**: (Smith et al., 2020) / Smith et al. (2020). Use "et al." from the first citation onward. Note the period after "al" (not after "et").
- **Organizational author**: spell out the full name on first use; may introduce an abbreviation in brackets for subsequent use. First citation: (World Health Organization [WHO], 2020). Subsequent citations: (WHO, 2020). Do not create abbreviations for organizations not commonly known by one.
- **No credited author**: use the first few words of the title in the author position. Use quotation marks for article/chapter titles: ("Study Finds," 2019). Use italics for book/report/website titles: (*Longitudinal Study*, 2019).
- **First authors with the same surname**: include given-name initials to distinguish, even if years differ: G. R. Smith et al. (2001) and T. J. Smith and Gladwell (2012).
- **Same first authors and same years (3+ author works)**: extend the surname lists to show the difference: (Parnell, Foster, et al., 2018; Parnell, Klein, et al., 2018).

#### Dates
- **No date**: (Smith, n.d.).
- **In press**: (Smith, in press).
- **Circa**: (Smith, ca. 2010).
- **Republished/translated work**: include both years separated by a slash: (Freud, 1905/1989).
- **Same author, same year**: use alphabetical suffixes: (Murakami, 2003a, 2003b).

#### Quotations and Page Numbers
- **Direct quote (under 40 words)**: integrate in text with quotation marks. Include page/paragraph number: (Smith, 2020, p. 15) or (Smith, 2020, para. 3). Use "pp." for page ranges: (pp. 124-125).
- **Direct quote (40+ words)**: format as a block quotation (indented 0.5 in, no quotation marks). Page number follows the closing punctuation of the block.
- **Narrative citation with quote**: Author (Year) states "quote" (p. 15).
- **Italicize scale anchors or response labels** rather than putting them in quotation marks (e.g., ranging from *strongly agree* to *strongly disagree*). Use quotation marks when reproducing exact test items or participant instructions.

#### Special Citation Types
- **Secondary source** (citing a source found in another source): use "as cited in." Only the source you actually read gets a reference list entry. Parenthetical: (Seldin, 1993, as cited in Pounder, 2007). Narrative: Seldin (1993, as cited in Pounder, 2007).
- **Personal communication** (emails, interviews, private letters, phone calls): cite in text only; no reference list entry. Include initials: (T. J. Adeyemi, personal communication, September 3, 2014).
- **"See" and "see also" citations**: use "see" to list example sources from a larger body of work: (see, e.g., Bloom et al., 1956; Cashin, 1990). Use "see also" for additional relevant sources: (Hashim & Zainol, 2015; see also Baider et al., 1994; Cohen & Fried, 2007).
- **"For more" citation**: (see Menzies et al., 2014, for more).

#### Multiple Citations in One Parenthetical
- Order alphabetically by first author surname, separated by semicolons: (Abbas & Ludden, 2018; Carlyle, 2015; Piquant, 2019).
- If multiple works by the same author, list years chronologically after a single author name: (Carlyle, 1997, 2015a, 2015b).
- Order within same author: n.d. first, then dated, then in press.

#### Citation Frequency
- The publication year is included with the first narrative citation in a paragraph but may be omitted from later narrative citations of the same source within the same paragraph. All parenthetical citations always include the year.

### Reference List Principles

#### Four Elements
Every reference entry has up to four elements: **Author**, **Date**, **Title**, **Source**.

#### Authors
- Surname, Initials. format. Periods after and spaces between initials. Suffixes (Jr., III) follow the initials.
- Commas separate all authors. Ampersand (&) before the final author.
- **1-20 authors**: list all authors.
- **21+ authors**: list the first 19, then an ellipsis (. . .), then the final author.

#### Dates
- Journals/books: (Year).
- Magazines/newspapers: (Year, Month Day) or (Year, Month).
- No date: (n.d.).
- In press: (in press).

#### Titles
- **Article and chapter titles**: sentence case, roman (not italic). Capitalize only the first word, the first word after a colon, and proper nouns.
- **Book, report, and standalone titles**: sentence case, *italic*.
- **Journal/magazine/newspaper names**: *italic*, title case (headline capitalization).
- Non-English titles: retain original language capitalization. Include an English translation in square brackets after the title: [English translation of the title].
- Volume number: *italic*. Issue number: roman, in parentheses immediately after volume. Include the issue number even if the journal is continuously paginated.

#### Source and Retrieval
- Include DOI when available, formatted as a URL: https://doi.org/xxxxx
- For works without a DOI accessed from a standard academic database, no URL or database name is needed.
- Include a URL for web-based sources without a DOI.
- "Retrieved [date] from" is used ONLY for content that changes frequently (e.g., live data dashboards, wikis). Most references do not need a retrieval date.
- If a nonperiodical resource is authored and published by the same organization, the publisher name is omitted (do not write "Author" in the publisher position).

### Reference Patterns by Source Type

#### Journal Articles
- **With DOI**: Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
- **Without DOI (print/database)**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), pages.
- **Advance online publication**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*. Advance online publication. https://doi.org/xxxxx
- **In press**: Author, A. A. (in press). Title of article. *Journal Name*.
- **Article with e-location number**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue), Article ######. https://doi.org/xxxxx
- **Article from supplement**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Suppl. #), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
- **Special issue (cited in full)**: Editor, A. A. (Ed.). (Year). Title of special issue [Special issue]. *Journal Name*, *Volume*(Issue).

#### Books
- **Authored book**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book*. Publisher.
- **Authored book with edition**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book* (2nd ed.). Publisher.
- **Edited book**: Editor, A. A. (Ed.). (Year). *Title of book*. Publisher. (Use "Eds." for multiple editors.)
- **Authored book with an editor**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book* (E. E. Editor, Ed.). Publisher.
- **Republished book**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book*. Publisher. (Original work published Year)
- **Translated book**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book* (T. T. Translator, Trans.; 2nd ed.). Publisher. (Original work published Year)
- **Book with volume**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of multivolume work: Vol. #. Volume title* (3rd ed.). Publisher.

#### Book Chapters
- **One editor**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of book* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
- **Two editors**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In A. A. Editor & B. B. Editor (Eds.), *Title of book* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
- **Three+ editors**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In A. A. Editor, B. B. Editor, & C. C. Editor (Eds.), *Title of book* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
- **Chapter in a volume with edition**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of book* (2nd ed., Vol. 1, pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
- **No credited author (encyclopedia entry)**: Title of the entry. (Year). In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of reference book* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.

#### Conference Presentations
- **Serial proceedings (published in a journal)**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of paper. *Proceedings Title*, *Volume*(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
- **Nonserial proceedings (published as a book)**: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of paper. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), *Title of proceedings* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
- **Paper presentation**: Presenter, A. A. (Year, Month Day-Day). *Title of paper* [Paper presentation]. Conference Name, City, State/Province, Country.
- **Poster presentation**: Presenter, A. A. (Year, Month Day-Day). *Title of poster* [Poster presentation]. Conference Name, City, Country.

#### Dissertations and Theses
- **Unpublished**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of dissertation* [Unpublished doctoral dissertation]. Institution Name.
- **From a database**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of dissertation* (Publication No. ######) [Doctoral dissertation, Institution Name]. Database Name.
- **From institutional repository**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of thesis* [Master's thesis, Institution Name]. Repository Name. https://URL

#### Reports
- **With report number**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. xxx). Publisher. https://URL
- **Organizational author same as publisher**: Organization Name. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. xxx). https://URL  [Omit publisher because it is the same as the author.]
- **Government department**: Department Name. (Year). *Title of report* (Series No. xxx). https://URL

#### Newspapers
- **Print**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Newspaper Name*, pages.
- **Online**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Newspaper Name*. https://URL
- **Editorial (no named author)**: Title of editorial [Editorial]. (Year, Month Day). *Newspaper Name*. https://URL

#### Magazines
- **Print with volume/issue**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Magazine Name*, *Volume*(Issue), pages.
- **Print without volume/issue**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month). Title of article. *Magazine Name*, pages.
- **Online**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Magazine Name*, *Volume*(Issue). https://URL

#### Web Sources
- **Webpage (individual author)**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. Site Name. https://URL
- **Webpage (organizational author same as site name)**: Organization. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. https://URL  [Omit site name because it duplicates the author.]
- **Webpage with no date**: Author, A. A. (n.d.). *Title of page*. Site Name. https://URL
- **Webpage with periodically updated content**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. Site Name. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from https://URL
- **Blog post**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of post. *Blog Name*. https://URL
- **Webpage on a news website**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Site Name*. https://URL

#### Social Media
- **Tweet / X post**: Author, A. A. [@handle]. (Year, Month Day). *First 20 words of post* [Post]. Platform. https://URL
- **Facebook post**: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *First 20 words of post* [Image attached]. Facebook. https://URL

#### Software, Apps, and Data Sets
- **Software (downloaded)**: Developer, A. A. (Year). *Title of software* (Version #.#) [Computer software]. https://URL
- **Mobile app**: Developer, A. A. (Year). *Title of app* (Version #.#) [Mobile app]. App Store/Google Play. https://URL
- **Data set**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of data set* [Data set]. Archive Name. https://doi.org/xxxxx

#### Audiovisual Sources
- **Film**: Director, A. A. (Director). (Year). *Title of film* [Film]. Production Company.
- **TV series**: Producer, A. A. (Executive Producer). (Year-Year). *Title of series* [TV series]. Production Company.
- **TV episode**: Writer, A. A. (Writer), & Director, A. A. (Director). (Year). Title of episode (Season #, Episode #) [TV series episode]. In A. A. Producer (Executive Producer), *Title of series*. Production Company.
- **YouTube video**: Creator, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of video* [Video]. YouTube. https://URL
- **Podcast episode**: Host, A. A. (Host). (Year, Month Day). Title of episode (No. #) [Audio podcast episode]. In *Title of podcast*. Production Company. https://URL
- **Webinar**: Presenter, A. A. (Year). *Title of webinar* [Webinar]. Organization. https://URL
- **Song**: Artist, A. A. (Year). Title of song [Song]. On *Title of album*. Label.

#### Unpublished Works
- **Unpublished manuscript**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of manuscript* [Unpublished manuscript]. Department Name, Institution Name.
- **Manuscript in preparation**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of manuscript* [Manuscript in preparation]. Department Name, Institution Name.
- **Preprint**: Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of preprint*. Preprint Server Name. https://doi.org/xxxxx

#### Legal References (Selected)
- **U.S. Supreme Court case**: Name v. Name, Volume U.S. Page (Year). https://URL
- **U.K. Supreme Court case**: Name v. Name, U.K.S.C. Number (Year). (Jurisdiction). https://URL
- **U.S. federal statute**: Title of Statute, Volume U.S.C. § Section (Year). https://URL
- **Patent**: Inventor, A. A. (Year). *Title of patent* (Patent No. #,###,###). Patent Office. https://URL

## Statistical and Numerical Accuracy
- Use numerals for 10+ and words for 0-9, unless used with units of measurement (e.g., 5 mg), in statistical contexts, or to express ages, dates, times, scores, or percentages.
- Numbers that begin a sentence are always spelled out.
- Italicize non-Greek statistical symbols: *M*, *SD*, *p*, *F*, *t*, *d*, *N*, *n*, *r*, *R*², etc.
- Standard formats: *M* = 7.70, *SD* = 1.20, *p* < .05, *t*(34) = 2.10, *F*(1, 120) = 4.57.
- **No leading zero** for statistics that cannot exceed 1.0 (correlations, proportions, *p*-values): .05, not 0.05.
- **Use a leading zero** for statistics that can exceed 1.0 (e.g., effect sizes like Cohen's *d* = 0.55, or standard deviations).
- Round *p*-values to two or three decimal places. Report exact *p*-values (e.g., *p* = .03) rather than inequalities when possible. Use *p* < .001 for very small values (do not report *p* = .000).
- Use the multiplication sign (×), not the letter x, in expressions like 2 × 4 design.
- Present percentages with numerals: 5%, 10%.

## Output Format: Inline Editing Mode

When the user uploads a document (or pastes text) for APA proofreading, use this output format instead of returning a corrected version.

### Layout Architecture

Use a two-panel layout within the HTML artifact:

- **Document panel** (left, 65-70% width): the user's full draft with inline error badges
- **Sidebar panel** (right, 30-35% width): correction cards for each flagged issue, fixed-position and independently scrollable
- On mobile viewports (<768px), collapse the sidebar below the document as an expandable accordion, with a sticky floating badge count at the top that toggles the panel open/closed
- Use responsive design throughout (fluid typography, adaptive containers, no fixed widths)

### Annotation Summary

At the top of the artifact, spanning both panels, include a Grammarly-inspired dashboard strip:

- **Overall APA Score** (0-100) displayed as a circular gauge, colour-coded green (90+), amber (70-89), red (<70)
- **Category breakdown** with individual scores and issue counts, each colour-coded:
  - 🔴 **Correctness**: grammar, punctuation, spelling
  - 🔵 **APA Compliance**: heading levels, number rules, abbreviations, title/sentence case, formatting
  - 🟣 **Citations & References**: in-text format, reference list format, cross-matching
  - 🟠 **Style**: voice, conciseness, bias-free language, wordiness
  - 🟢 **Statistical Notation**: leading zeros, italicisation, p-value formatting
  - 🩵 **Tables & Figures**: border rules, labels, titles, notes, structure
- **One-line verdict** (e.g., "Score: 74 — 14 issues found: 5 critical, 9 advisory")
- Each category is clickable, filtering the sidebar to show only issues of that type

### Document Panel (Left)

- Place the **full draft** preserving all original paragraph structure and headings.
- **Inline error marking**: wrap each error in `<s style="color:#d32f2f; text-decoration:line-through">` immediately followed by the correction in `<span style="color:#2e7d32; font-weight:600">`.
- **Numbered badge**: place a small superscript badge after each correction (e.g., `<sup class="badge badge-red" data-issue="3">3</sup>`) colour-coded to its category. Clicking the badge scrolls the sidebar to the matching correction card and highlights it.
- Colour-code the left border of each correction span to match its dashboard category (red, blue, purple, orange, green, teal).
- **Long sentences** (roughly 40+ words or multiple independent clauses): insert a brief inline note below the sentence in muted style (e.g., `<div style="color:#666; font-size:0.85em; margin:4px 0 8px 1em;">💡 Consider splitting: [brief suggestion]</div>`), with its own numbered badge linking to a sidebar card.
- **Tables with issues**: render the table exactly as the user wrote it (preserving their incorrect borders, shading, missing labels, etc.) but wrap the entire table in a coloured outline (`border: 2px solid #00838f`) and place a numbered badge at the top-right corner of the table. Do not attempt inline strikethrough corrections inside tables with structural problems.
- **Tables without issues**: render in correct APA style (three-rule borders, bold label, italic title, flush-left note) with no badge.

### Sidebar Panel (Right)

Each flagged issue gets a **correction card** in the sidebar, ordered by position in the document. Each card contains:

1. **Issue number and category badge** (colour-coded dot + label, e.g., "🔴 Correctness")
2. **Severity tag**: Critical, Major, or Minor
3. **What's wrong**: brief plain-language description of the error
4. **APA rule**: the specific rule or section reference (e.g., "APA 7th, Section 6.12 — Serial Comma")
5. **Correction**: the suggested fix. For text issues, show the corrected phrase. For tables and figures, show a **mini preview of the correctly formatted version** rendered in proper APA style within the card
6. **Context snippet**: a short excerpt of the surrounding sentence for orientation

Card interaction:

- Clicking a card scrolls the document panel to the corresponding badge and briefly highlights it
- Cards can be filtered by category using the dashboard strip buttons
- Cards can be filtered by severity (Critical / Major / Minor toggle)
- Hovering over a card in the sidebar highlights the corresponding badge in the document panel (desktop only)

### Table and Figure Correction Cards

When a table has structural APA violations, the sidebar card includes:

- **"Your table"**: a thumbnail or compact rendering of the table as written, with the problems annotated (e.g., red arrows pointing to vertical borders, a callout noting the missing label)
- **"APA format"**: a correctly formatted version of the same table using the three-rule border pattern, bold flush-left label, italic flush-left title, and flush-left *Note.* if applicable
- **Checklist of issues**: each violation listed with its specific APA rule (e.g., "Remove vertical borders — APA 7th, Section 7.8", "Add bold table number — APA 7th, Section 7.10")

For figures, the card notes labelling and formatting errors and shows the expected label/title/note structure, but does not attempt to render the figure image itself.

### Categories to Flag

1. **Correctness** 🔴: grammar, subject-verb agreement, pronoun issues, spelling, punctuation (serial comma, hyphenation, quotation marks, block-quote formatting)
2. **APA Compliance** 🔵: heading levels, number rules, abbreviation rules, title case vs sentence case, page layout, title page elements
3. **Citations & References** 🟣: in-text citation format errors, missing components, et al. misuse, reference list formatting, cross-matching between in-text citations and reference entries
4. **Style** 🟠: wordiness, redundancy, hedging, colloquialisms, passive voice overuse, bias-free language violations, person-first violations, outdated terms, "subjects" instead of "participants"
5. **Statistical Notation** 🟢: leading zeros, italicisation of symbols, p-value formatting, confidence intervals, effect size reporting
6. **Tables & Figures** 🩵: missing or malformatted labels/titles/notes, vertical borders, cell shading, incorrect numbering, missing table/figure cross-references in text

### Scoring Guidelines

Calculate the overall score by starting at 100 and deducting points per issue:

- **Critical issues** (deduct 3 points each): missing or malformed references, incorrect heading hierarchy, statistical notation errors, citation/reference mismatches, tables missing labels entirely
- **Major issues** (deduct 2 points each): wrong verb tense for context, number rule violations, bias-free language violations, missing serial commas, table structural violations (borders, shading)
- **Minor issues** (deduct 1 point each): wordiness, passive voice, minor punctuation, capitalisation inconsistencies, missing italic on table title

Floor the score at 0. Category scores are calculated the same way within each category, normalised to 100.

### What NOT to Do

- Do not rewrite the passage for the user.
- Do not add content, expand arguments, or reorganise sections.
- Do not flag stylistic preferences that are outside APA rules.
- Do not correct tables silently in the document panel; always show the user's original and present the correction in the sidebar card.
